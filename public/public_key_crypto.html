Cryptographic algorithms depend on the fact that prime factorization of large numbers takes a very very very long time üêå to calculate.

Let's look at how one of the most popular algorithms works. RSA.


You start by picking two prime numbers. A prime number is a number that is only divisible by itself.

In a real world example you would pick larger number but for the sake of keeping this simple we're going to use small ones.

We pick 2 and 7. Let's call these P and Q.

It's easy to calculate the product of these two numbers. 2 * 7. You can even do this by hand, and you'll reach the value of 14. We'll call this N and we'll use this as part of our public key numbers.

But it's harder to do the reverse operation. What are the values of P and Q for when P * Q = 14?

This is increasingly difficult as the numbers get longer, and it's difficult for both humans and machines.

Before we continue we need to generate another number, L.

The rules to create this number tell us to subtract one from P and Q and multiply them. So we take one off 2 and 7 and multiplied we get L = (2 - 1) * (7 - 1) = 6.

Now we get to pick the second number (E) for our public key. It has to be between 1 and L (6) and coprime with L (6) and N (14).

There's only one possible option, 5, so we pick that one and we have our public key of N 14 and E 5 that we can use to encrypt messages.

To decrypt messages, we need yet one other number, D.

D has to follow one rule, which is D * E modulus the length of the non common factor has to be 1.

Sounds complicated and it is. D (?) * E (5) mod( (P (2) - 1) (Q (7) - 1) ) = 1

So... D (?) * (5) mod( 1 * 6 ) = 1

Solving for D, D = 11 where 11 * 5 % 6 = 1

That's it! You have setup your encryption system! Let's send our message üì´!


Let's recap the numbers we have for our keys.

E (5) and N (14) for the public key, and D (11) and N (14) for the private key.


Imagine you want to tell someone how much $ you have in your account but you don't want anyone else to know.

You grab your balance, which let's assume it's $2 and multiply it by itself E number of times (2 to the power of E).

E is 5 so this means 2 * 2 * 2 * 2 * 2 (5 times) which is a really large number 32.

We then divide this number by N (14). The result of this is 2 with a remainder of 4.

We then send 4 to the person we want to tell our balance. We don't care if someone sees this message because they will not be able to decrypt it.

But how does the other person decrypt it? How do they go from this weird 4 back to $2?

Well. Easy.

We take 4 to the power of D (11) modulus N (14).

This means 4 ^ 11 mod(14).

4 to the power of 11 is 4,194,304. This mod 14 is 2.
